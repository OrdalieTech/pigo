   and wire entity are deleted; Google Chat's preview mutex/state/throttle/config and preview-only
   truncation helper are deleted. Uncommitted legacy compatibility is absent: current Google
   `spaceType` and PKCS#8 credentials only, and Discord's structured mentions only.
2. **Dependency audit:** `go mod verify` and `go mod tidy -diff` are clean; `go.mod`/`go.sum` are
   unchanged. Package import enumeration shows only standard-library and in-module dependencies.
3. **Duplication sweep:** the sole `dupl` group was the byte-identical Discord/Messenger readable
   rune splitter. It moved to `chat/internal/runechunk`; a second scan reports zero groups.
4. **Abstraction audit:** the new packages define no interfaces. They implement the frozen
   `chat.Adapter`/`chat.Delivery` seams directly; transport helpers are concrete and have multiple
   consumers.
5. **LOC report:** the absolute addition LOC is recorded above. The ≤1.3× mirror budget is not
   applicable because D28 has no upstream TS counterpart; `docs/compare/sprint-6.md` records the
   reference comparison instead.
6. **Size/speed trend:** the chat SDK packages are outside the CLI link graph; the measured binary
   and startup therefore carry no Sprint 6 product cost.
7. **Milestone verification:** every Sprint 6 definition-of-done item in `SPRINTS.md` is covered by
   deterministic fake-server/gateway tests and the full race gate. Existing M1–M5 owner-gated
   items remain accurately open in `PROGRESS.md`; this addition neither weakens nor closes them.
8. **Upstream alignment:** chat is an explicit divergence-ledger addition. The Hermes/pi-chat and
   official-wire cross-check is green in `docs/compare/sprint-6.md`; no upstream fixture or product
   asset changed.
